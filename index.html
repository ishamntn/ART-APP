Why This Advocacy?

Our planet faces unprecedented threats—from rising seas to vanishing wildlife. I chose Environmental Conservation & Climate Change Awareness because art has the power to move hearts and minds: it can translate complex data into emotion and spur us to act.

In this virtual gallery you’ll see eight works—each born from urgent environmental issues. Together they tell a story of loss, resilience, and hope.

Dolphin Mural #1

Artist: A.G. Saño | Medium: Outdoor mural painting | Year: 2010

Painted in response to the slaughter of dolphins in Taiji (Japan), it launched Saño’s “Whale Boy” campaign to raise marine‐life awareness across the Philippines. rare.org, lifestyle.inquirer.net

  • Line & Shape: Fluid dolphin silhouettes create a sense of school‐movement.
  • Color: Deep blues & bright whites evoke ocean light.
  • Texture: Flat paint on rough concrete adds rawness.
  • Balance & Rhythm: Repetition establishes visual flow.
  • Emphasis: A larger dolphin anchors the gaze.

Tropical Climate Forensics

Artist: Derek Tumala | Medium: Interactive digital installation | Year: 2022

Uses historical meteorological data to let visitors “time-travel” through key climate events, making the crisis tangible. vogue.ph

  • Space & Movement: 3D diorama guides users through atmospheric layers.
  • Color: Heat‐map reds/oranges vs. cool blues denote shifts.
  • Texture: Glowing grids mimic scientific instrumentation.
  • Contrast & Emphasis: Hotspots draw attention to critical data.
  • Unity: Consistent UI motifs tie modules together.

Mebuyan’s Colony

Artist: Leeroy New | Medium: Bamboo & recycled plastic installation | Year: 2022

A walk-through “colony” blending agro-spaces and waste to explore food security under climate stress. vogue.ph

  • Shape: Pod-like bamboo frames echo seeds.
  • Texture: Rough bamboo vs. smooth plastic highlights waste.
  • Color: Natural hues vs. muted greens call for reuse.
  • Pattern & Rhythm: Bottle clusters pulse like ecosystems.
  • Emphasis: Central “harvest” nook focuses on gathering.

Tigpanalipod (The Protectors)

Artist: Martha Atienza | Medium: Video installation | Year: 2022

Chronicles fishers’ custodial bond with the sea—underscoring local stewardship amid rising tides. ocula.com, vogue.ph

  • Line & Movement: Slow pans mirror gentle waves.
  • Color: Muted pastels reinforce authenticity.
  • Space: Immersive scale blurs gallery & seascape.
  • Contrast: Calm vs. storm footage heightens impact.
  • Unity: Nets, boats & water weave a cohesive narrative.

Dam Nation

Artist: Cian Dayrit | Medium: Acrylic & mixed-media on canvas | Year: 2023

Depicts waterways severed by dams—linking colonial infrastructure to climate vulnerability. artmonthly.co.uk

  • Shape: Jagged peaks cut by straight “blades.”
  • Color: Earth tones vs. stark whites show discord.
  • Texture: Layered paint & collage evoke sediment.
  • Balance & Contrast: Symmetry vs. disruption.
  • Emphasis: Fractured river path focuses attention.

The Passage of Storms

Artist: Gab Mejia | Medium: Documentary photography series | Year: 2023

An elegiac narrative of coastal communities after Super Typhoon Rai (“Odette”), preserving memories of loss and resilience. vogue.ph

  • Line & Shape: Fractured lines frame human subjects.
  • Color: Desaturated palettes evoke gloom.
  • Texture: Gritty grain highlights scars.
  • Contrast: Shadow vs. light underscores hope vs. despair.
  • Rhythm: Sequenced images move from ruin to repair.

State of Plantocracy

Artist: Cian Dayrit | Medium: Hand-embroidered textile | Year: 2023

Embroidery maps monoculture power structures—exposing how extractive agriculture fuels disparity and ecological harm. climate.gov.ph

  • Line & Pattern: Dense threads map plantation grids.
  • Color: Muted greens & browns vs. bright resistance threads.
  • Texture: Raised stitches invite tactile inspection.
  • Contrast & Emphasis: Disrupted grids highlight struggle.
  • Unity: Consistent stitches unify symbolic references.
